What.

What is our platform about?

Buying from huge e-commerce websites such as Amazon has many advantages, but paradoxically, users are often confused by the vast variety of products that are offered. Users may have a rough idea about the characteristics of the product they want to buy, but they often undergo the same process of comparing similar products. We aim to remove this redundancy and aid them in their purchases, suggesting the best or most popular products that correspond to their search. For instance, comparing smartphones or laptops may be difficult due to the wide price range and the required technical knowledge. With our platform, customers can easily visualise in a graphical way what products match their needs, and among them, the most popular ones.

Why.

Why is our solution effective?

When you look at a product on Amazon, I am sure you have noticed at least once a small window at the bottom of the page that indicates what other products users usually buy instead. We call it a competitive relation. What do we do is just to build a graph of these relations among all products of each category. If a product is associated with many of these relations, it means that users have preferred it many times over the others. This new way of defining popular products allows us to group them according to human-made criteria: the brand, the price or some peculiar characteristics. But it is decided by humans , not just algorithms. Just like you.

Who.

Who should use our platform?

With our platform we want to aid both customers and vendors. Customers may take advantage of previous people's choices to decide what product is worth buying, or to get an idea about alternative items. Vendors may explore the visualization to search competitors and see the characteristics of the most popular items in their category.

We should put a kind of tutorial here.

Categories graph

We first create a graph to display all categories of the products, their hierarchical relations and the amount of products of a specific category. It helps the user have a intuitive and immediate view both of the type and the amount products offered by Amazon.

Part 2

Products graph

The objective of the project is to show the relations among the products. A search bar will allow the user to search and visualize the goods they want to analyze. Thanks to this graph a customer can intuitively identify which product is the best while a vendor could verify whether its product is preferred or not to the other ones.
The red nodes represent products while the blue nodes represent a cluster of competing products. The arrows point in the direction of the preferred product.
